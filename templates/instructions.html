This study is comprised of a short task and some questions. It's conducted by researchers at Royal Holloway, University of London (UK). Our goal is to learn more about decision-making. It should take about 5 minutes to complete.


Your participation is voluntary and your responses are anonymous. No one will be able to track your answers back to you.

This is an experimental study about decision-making. On top of the ${{fixed_payment}} for completing the study, you can receive an additional payment based on your choices. This additional payment will only be available to participants that correctly answer a few comprehension questions prior to the start of the study.


In the following pages we will explain the experimental task, how you make decisions, and how the extra payment is determined.


Any extra payment will be deposited as a bonus on MTurk within 3 days of of the end of the experiment.

This study consists of several choice problems where you will choose between different investment portfolios. One of the choice problems will be randomly selected, and your payment will be determined by the portforlio you choose in that problem.


A portfolio has two components,

  • A cash component: this is an amount of money you will get for sure.

  • A share of a stock option: payment assocated with the stock option depends on the performance of {{symbol}}, which tracks the preformance of the Dow Jones Industrial Average stock market index.

The stock option's value will be calculated in {{delay}} days, on {{executeTime}} (after markets close, if applicable).

The value of the stock option will be the difference between the current value of the stock (now) and its value at the time of calculation (in {{delay}} days), or 0, which ever is larger. When choosing a portfolio, you will have acess to the current value of {{symbol}} as well as a chart with the index's preformance over then last 100 days.
To reiterate,

  • If the share price of {{symbol}} increases over the next {{delay}} days, the option is worth the amount of the increase.

  • If the share price of {{symbol}} decreases over the next {{delay}} days, the option is worth $0.00. You cannot lose money by holding the option.
For example, consider the portfolio , with a cash value of $2.50 and 0.8 shares of the stock option. If

  • If the share price of {{symbol}} increases by $5.50, the portfolio's stock option is worth 0.8*$5.50 = $4.40, so the portfolio's total value is: $2.50 + $4.40 = $6.90.

  • If the share price of {{symbol}} decreases by $3.20, the portfolio's stock option is worth $0.00, so the portfolio's total value is: $2.50.


Again, consider the portfolio , with as cash value of $4.00 and 0.5 shares of the stock option. If

  • If the share price of {{symbol}} increases by $2.70, the portfolio's stock option is worth 0.5*$2.70 = $1.35, so the portfolio's total value is: $4.00 + $1.35 = $5.35.

  • If the share price of {{symbol}} decreases by $1.90, the portfolio's stock option is worth $0.00, so the portfolio's total value is: $4.00.


When choosing bewteen portfolios, you can view the performance of {{symbol}} over the last 100 days by clicking on the "CHART" button.

By hovering your mouse over the chart, you can see what the value of one share of the stock option would have been worth at each date.
